Tell government about a death

When someone dies, any allowances or benefits they received will need to be cancelled.

As soon as you can, you'll need to give some details about the person who has died. This will help to prevent overpayment of any benefits they received and fraudulent use of their identity.

You'll be able to see which details will change before you confirm you are ready to pass this information on.

What you'll need

  • The National Insurance number or the date of birth of the person who died
  • The date they died
  • The permission of the next of kin or executor if you're giving this information on their behalf, and their contact details
