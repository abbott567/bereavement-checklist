Tell government about a death

When someone dies, any allowances or benefits they received will need to be cancelled.

As soon as you can, you'll need to give some details about the person who has died. This will help to prevent overpayment of any benefits they received and fraudulent use of their identity.

You'll be able to see which details will change before you confirm you are ready to pass this information on.

Benefits you could get

You'll be asked for some personal details and about your relationship to the person who died. This is to find out if you could get Bereavement Support Payment. You can also get information about help with funeral costs.

You can give these details on behalf of the person dealing with the estate. You must have their permission to do this.

What you'll need

  • The National Insurance number or the date of birth of the person who died
  • The date they died
  • The contact details of the person dealing with the estate, if you're giving this information on their behalf
