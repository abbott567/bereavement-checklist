Tell us someone has died

When someone dies, any State Pension, allowances or benefits they received will need to be cancelled.

It's understandable that telling government organisations about a death is a lower priority than the many other arrangements that need to be made.

However, as soon as possible, you'll need to give some details about the person who has died. This will help to prevent the overpayment of any benefits and the fraudulent use of their identity.

Once you've finished, you'll be able to see which organisations have been contacted and what will be stopped.

You'll also be able to find out about help with funeral costs or other benefits.

What you'll need

  • The National Insurance number or the date of birth of the person who died
  • The date they died

You don't have to be the next of kin to give this information - but you must have their permission before you start. You'll also need their date of birth and contact details.
