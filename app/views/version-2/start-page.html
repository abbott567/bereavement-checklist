Tell us someone has died

When someone dies, any State Pension, allowances or benefits they received will need to be cancelled.

You'll need to give some details about the person who has died. This should only take a few minutes and will help to prevent overpayment of benefits and the fraudulent use of their identity documents.

Once you've finished, you'll be able to see which organisations have been contacted and what they will do with the information you provide. You'll also be able to find out about help with funeral costs or other benefits.

What you'll need

  • The National Insurance number or the date of birth of the person who died
  • The date they died

You don't have to be the next of kin to give this information - but you must have their permission before you start. You'll also need their date of birth and contact details.
